Set up your account and create your first AI-powered study session in 10 minutes

What is NotebookLM?

NotebookLM is Google's AI-powered research and study tool. Unlike ChatGPT, which draws from its training data, NotebookLM works exclusively with materials you upload. This means:

  • Responses are grounded in your actual course materials
  • No hallucinated information from outside sources
  • Citations point to specific parts of your documents
  • Audio overviews discuss your uploaded content, not generic summaries
Key Difference from ChatGPT

ChatGPT knows a lot about everything. NotebookLM knows everything about what you upload. For medical students, this means NotebookLM won't mix up information from different sources or give you answers based on outdated guidelines.

Step 1: Create Your Account

  1. Go to NotebookLM Visit notebooklm.google.com in your browser.
  2. Sign in with Google Use your Google account (personal or school). NotebookLM is free to use.
  3. Accept the Terms Review and accept Google's terms of service to continue.

Step 2: Create Your First Notebook

A "notebook" in NotebookLM is a container for related source materials. Think of it like a course folder.

  1. Click "New Notebook" You'll see this button on the main dashboard after signing in.
  2. Name Your Notebook Use descriptive names like "Cardiology - Heart Failure" or "Step 1 - Renal System"
Organization Tip

Create separate notebooks for each major topic rather than one giant notebook. This keeps audio overviews focused and makes it easier to find specific information.

Step 3: Upload Your Source Materials

NotebookLM can work with several types of files:

File Type Best For
PDF Lecture slides, textbook chapters, journal articles
Google Docs Your own notes, study guides
Text files Copied content, outlines
Web URLs Online resources, guidelines (use sparingly)
  1. Click "Add Source" This button appears in the left sidebar of your notebook.
  2. Upload Your Files Drag and drop or browse to select files. Start with 3-5 related documents.
  3. Wait for Processing NotebookLM takes a moment to analyze your sources. You'll see a progress indicator.
Common Mistake

Don't upload too many sources at once. Start with 3-5 focused documents. Too many sources dilute the quality of responses and make audio overviews too general.

Step 4: Generate Your First Audio Overview

Audio Overviews are one of NotebookLM's most powerful features‚Äîa podcast-style discussion of your uploaded materials.

  1. Find the Audio Overview Section Look for the "Audio Overview" or "Notebook Guide" section in your notebook.
  2. Click "Generate" NotebookLM will create a conversational audio summary. This takes 2-5 minutes.
  3. Listen and Learn The audio features two AI hosts discussing your content in an engaging, accessible way.

Step 5: Start Chatting with Your Sources

Beyond audio, you can ask questions and get answers grounded in your materials.

  1. Use the Chat Feature Type questions in the chat area at the bottom of your notebook.
  2. Get Cited Responses NotebookLM shows which sources support each answer. Click citations to see the original text.
  3. Try Different Prompts Ask for explanations, comparisons, practice questions, or study guides.

Common Questions

Is NotebookLM free?
Yes, NotebookLM is currently free to use with a Google account. There are usage limits, but they're generous enough for typical student use.
Can I use it on my phone?
NotebookLM works in mobile browsers, though the experience is optimized for desktop. Audio overviews are great for listening on mobile.
Is my data private?
Your uploaded sources are not used to train Google's AI models. Review Google's privacy policy for complete details.
How is this different from uploading to ChatGPT?
NotebookLM only uses your uploaded materials, never its training data. ChatGPT may blend your materials with other information. NotebookLM also provides citations and creates audio overviews.
What if I can't find a feature?
NotebookLM updates frequently. If something looks different, the core workflow remains: upload sources, generate audio, and chat with your materials.
