Universal Prompts

Practice questions, flashcards, mnemonics, and pharmacology prompts that work for everyone

These prompts work regardless of your learning style or training stage. They're useful additions to any study session.

Practice Questions

Multiple Choice Questions

Best for: Self-testing, exam simulation

Generate 10 multiple-choice questions about [topic] at the level of [Step 1/Step 2/shelf exam]. Include the correct answer and brief explanations for each.

Clinical Vignettes

Best for: Board-style practice

Create 5 clinical vignette-style questions about [condition]. Each should include patient presentation, relevant findings, and ask about diagnosis or next best step in management.

Two-Step Questions

Best for: Advanced reasoning practice

Create 5 two-step reasoning questions about [topic]. The first step should require identifying the diagnosis, and the second step should ask about management or mechanism.

Flashcards

Concept Flashcards

Best for: Vocabulary and definitions

Create 20 flashcard pairs for [topic]. Front should have the term/concept, back should have the definition and clinical relevance.

Clinical Presentation Cards

Best for: Pattern recognition

Generate flashcards with classic presentations of [conditions]. Front: clinical vignette, Back: diagnosis and key distinguishing features.

Image-Based Cards

Best for: Visual diagnosis

Create flashcards describing classic imaging/lab/physical exam findings for [conditions]. Front: describe what would be seen, Back: diagnosis and significance.

Mnemonics and Memory Aids

Mnemonic Creation

Best for: Lists and sequences

Create a mnemonic to remember [list of items/criteria/steps]. Make it memorable and explain what each letter stands for.

Story-Based Memory

Best for: Complex syndromes

Create a short, memorable story that incorporates all the key features of [condition/syndrome]. Each story element should represent a clinical finding or fact.

Association Chains

Best for: Connecting related facts

Create a chain of associations linking [concept A] to [concept B] to [concept C]. Each link should be memorable and clinically meaningful.

Pharmacology

Drug Overview

Best for: Learning individual drugs

Provide a comprehensive overview of [drug]. Include: mechanism of action, indications, dosing considerations, major side effects, contraindications, and drug interactions.

Drug Class Comparison

Best for: Understanding drug families

Compare all drugs in the [drug class]. Create a table showing similarities and differences in mechanism, uses, and side effects.

Mechanism to Side Effects

Best for: Understanding why drugs cause specific effects

Explain how the mechanism of [drug/drug class] leads to both its therapeutic effects and its side effects. Help me understand the logic connecting mechanism to clinical effects.

Drug Interactions

Best for: Clinical safety

List the most important drug interactions for [drug/drug class]. Explain the mechanism of each interaction and clinical consequences.

General Study Aids

Explain Like I'm Five

Best for: Simplifying complex topics

Explain [complex topic] in the simplest possible terms, as if to someone with no medical background. Then explain it again using proper medical terminology.

Common Misconceptions

Best for: Avoiding errors

What are the most common misconceptions or mistakes students make about [topic]? Explain each misconception and the correct understanding.

Clinical Pearls

Best for: Practical wisdom

What are the most important clinical pearls about [topic] that experienced clinicians know but textbooks don't always emphasize?
Pro Tip

After NotebookLM generates flashcards, copy them into a spaced repetition app like Anki. The combination of NotebookLM's content generation with Anki's scheduling algorithm is incredibly powerful for long-term retention.
