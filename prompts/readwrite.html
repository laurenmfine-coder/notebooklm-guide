Basic Prompts - Study Guides

Comprehensive Study Guide

Best for: Initial learning, complete topic coverage

Create a comprehensive study guide for [topic] that includes: key definitions, pathophysiology, clinical presentation, diagnosis, and management. Organize it in a logical flow.

One-Page Summary

Best for: Quick review, exam cramming

Generate a one-page study sheet for [topic] with only the most essential information I need to know. Use bullet points and keep it concise.

Exam-Focused Guide

Best for: Targeted exam preparation

Create a study guide for [topic] focused on what's most commonly tested on [specific exam]. Include classic presentations and high-yield facts.

Chapter Summary

Best for: Processing textbook material

Summarize the key points from these sources about [topic]. Organize by major concepts and include important details under each.

Advanced Prompts - Study Materials

Systems-Based Review

Best for: Organ system approach, comprehensive coverage

Create a study guide organized by body system for [topic]. For each system, include: normal physiology, pathophysiology, clinical manifestations, and relevant treatments.

Integrated Study Sheet

Best for: Cross-course integration, connecting concepts

Generate a study guide that integrates [topic] across multiple courses. Include the relevant anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pharmacology, and pathology. Show how they connect.

Clinical Pearls Sheet

Best for: Rotation preparation, practical tips

Create a "clinical pearls" study sheet for [rotation/specialty]. Include practical tips, key physical exam findings, common presentations, and things attendings expect you to know on rounds.

Weak Area Focus

Best for: Targeting knowledge gaps

Based on my uploaded materials about [topic], create a focused study guide on the most complex or commonly misunderstood aspects. Include detailed explanations and examples.

Practice Questions and Flashcards

Multiple Choice Questions

Best for: Self-testing, exam simulation

Generate 10 multiple-choice questions about [topic] at the level of [Step 1/Step 2/shelf exam]. Include the correct answer and brief explanations for each.

Clinical Vignettes

Best for: Board-style practice

Create 5 clinical vignette-style questions about [condition]. Each should include patient presentation, relevant findings, and ask about diagnosis or next best step in management.

Concept Flashcards

Best for: Memorization, spaced repetition

Create 20 flashcard pairs for [topic]. Front should have the term/concept, back should have the definition and clinical relevance.

Clinical Presentation Cards

Best for: Pattern recognition, diagnosis practice

Generate flashcards with classic presentations of [conditions]. Front: clinical vignette, Back: diagnosis and key distinguishing features.

Memory Aids

Mnemonic Creation

Create a mnemonic to remember [list of items/criteria/steps]. Make it memorable and explain what each letter stands for.

Story-Based Memory Aid

Create a short, memorable story that incorporates all the key features of [condition/syndrome]. Each story element should represent a clinical finding or fact.
Pro Tip

Don't just read what NotebookLM generates. Rewrite key sections in your own words. The "generation effect" in memory research shows that producing text yourself leads to far better retention than passive reading.
