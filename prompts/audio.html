Audio Learner Prompts

Prompts optimized for audio overviews and listening-based study

These prompts help you get the most out of NotebookLM's Audio Overview feature and create content optimized for listening.

Basic Prompts

General Topic Overview

Best for: Initial learning, getting oriented to a new topic

Create an audio overview explaining [topic] in a conversational style, as if two medical students are discussing it over coffee. Focus on the key concepts I need to understand for my exam.

Quick Review Audio

Best for: Pre-exam review, refreshing previously learned material

Create a 10-15 minute audio overview covering the highest-yield points about [topic] for quick review before my exam.

Case-Based Discussion

Best for: Clinical application, preparing for rotations

Generate an audio overview that walks through clinical cases involving [condition]. Include how to approach diagnosis and management, with explanations of the reasoning.

Advanced Prompts

Compare and Contrast

Best for: Distinguishing similar conditions, differential diagnosis

Create an audio overview that compares and contrasts [condition A] and [condition B]. Focus on how to differentiate them clinically, including presentation, diagnostic findings, and management differences.

Mechanism-Focused

Best for: Understanding pathophysiology, connecting basic science to clinical

Generate an audio discussion explaining the pathophysiology of [condition] from the cellular level up to clinical manifestations. Connect the basic science to clinical findings and treatment rationale.

Clinical Reasoning Audio

Best for: Developing diagnostic thinking, preparing for clinical rotations

Create an audio overview that walks through the clinical reasoning process for [chief complaint/symptom]. Include differential diagnosis generation, workup decisions, and interpretation of findings.

Integration Audio

Best for: Connecting concepts across courses, holistic understanding

Generate an audio overview that integrates anatomy, physiology, pathology, and pharmacology related to [organ system/condition]. Show how these basic sciences connect to clinical practice.

Exam Prep Audio

Best for: Board preparation, shelf exam review

Create an audio overview for [shelf exam/board exam topic] focusing on commonly tested concepts, classic presentations, and high-yield facts. Include memory aids where helpful.

Specialty-Specific Prompts

Pharmacology Focus

Create an audio discussion of [drug class] covering mechanism of action, major drugs in the class, clinical uses, and important side effects. Explain in a way that helps me remember the connections.

Anatomy Review

Generate an audio overview of [anatomical region] that walks through the structures, their relationships, and clinical relevance. Include common pathology affecting this area.

Microbiology Review

Create an audio discussion covering [organism/infection type]. Include characteristics, pathogenesis, clinical presentation, diagnosis, and treatment in a conversational format.
Pro Tip

For audio overviews, create separate notebooks for specific topics rather than uploading all your materials to one notebook. This keeps the audio focused and prevents overly general discussions.
