Training Stage Prompts

Prompts tailored to pre-clinical, clinical rotations, and board preparation

These prompts are designed for the specific needs and challenges of each stage of medical training.

Pre-Clinical (MS1/MS2) Prompts

Focus: Building foundational knowledge, understanding mechanisms, connecting basic science to clinical relevtic

Foundation Building

Best for: Initial learning of new topics

Explain [topic] starting from basic concepts, building to complex applications. Include why this matters clinically and how it connects to patient care.

Mechanism Deep Dive

Best for: Understanding pharmacology, pathophysiology

Explain the mechanism of action of [drug/process] at the molecular level, then show how this mechanism explains both the therapeutic effects AND the side effects.

Basic Science Integration

Best for: Connecting classroom to clinic

Show how [basic science concept] connects to clinical practice. Explain why understanding this concept matters for patient care and clinical decision-making.

Concept Connection

Best for: Cross-course integration

Explain how [concept from course A] relates to [concept from course B]. Show the connections that will help me integrate my learning across courses.

Clinical Rotations (MS3/MS4) Prompts

Focus: Clinical reasoning, practical application, presenting on rounds

Differential Diagnosis Builder

Best for: Clinical reasoning, approaching new patients

Generate a comprehensive differential diagnosis for a patient presenting with [symptom]. Organize by most common, most serious, and don't-miss diagnoses. Include key distinguishing features.

Clinical Reasoning Practice

Best for: Developing diagnostic thinking

Walk me through the clinical reasoning process for a patient presenting with [symptom]. Include: initial hypotheses, what history and exam would test these hypotheses, and how to interpret findings.

Pre-Rounds Prep

Best for: Preparing for morning rounds

For a patient with [condition], help me prepare for rounds by covering: key pathophysiology, typical presentation, diagnostic workup, management approach, and potential complications to monitor.

Presentation Practice

Best for: Improving oral presentations

Create a presentation script about [topic] as if I'm presenting on rounds. Include the key points to cover, how to organize the information, and anticipated questions attendings might ask.

Rotation-Specific Review

Best for: Quick reference during rotations

Create a quick reference guide for [specialty] rotation covering: common presentations, must-know emergencies, key medications, and what attendings typically expect students to know.

Board Preparation Prompts

Focus: High-yield facts, question patterns, rapid review

High-Yield Summary

Best for: Focused review of commonly tested topics

What are the top 10 most high-yield facts about [topic] for [specific exam]? Format as concise bullet points I can review quickly.

Question Pattern Recognition

Best for: Understanding how topics are tested

What are common ways [topic] is tested on [exam type]? Provide example question stems and explain what the question is really asking for.

Weak Spot Identification

Best for: Targeting areas needing improvement

Based on my uploaded materials, identify concepts within [topic] that are frequently misunderstood or incompletely explained. Provide clear explanations of these challenging areas.

Rapid Review

Best for: Last-minute review before exam

Create a rapid review guide for [topic] that I can read through in 15 minutes before my exam. Include only must-know information.

Classic Presentations

Best for: Pattern recognition on exams

List the classic/"buzzword" presentations for conditions related to [topic]. Include the key finding that should make me think of each diagnosis.

First-Order vs. Second-Order Questions

Best for: Understanding question complexity

For [topic], create examples of both first-order (recall) and second-order (application) questions. Explain the difference in how to approach each type.
Pro Tip

Adjust your prompts as you progress through training. Early on, focus on "explain why" prompts. During rotations, shift to "how do I approach" prompts. For boards, use "what's high-yield" and "how is this tested" prompts.
