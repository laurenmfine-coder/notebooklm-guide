Essential Prompts for Read/Write Learners

Comprehensive Study Guide

Create a comprehensive study guide for [topic] that includes: key definitions, pathophysiology, clinical presentation, diagnosis, and management. Organize it in a logical flow.

One-Page Summary

Generate a one-page study sheet for [topic] with only the most essential information I need to know. Use bullet points and keep it concise.

Practice Questions

Generate 10 multiple-choice questions about [topic] at the level of [Step 1/Step 2/shelf exam]. Include the correct answer and brief explanations for each.

Flashcard Content

Create 20 flashcard pairs for [topic]. Front should have the term/concept, back should have the definition and clinical relevance.

Common Questions

Should I just read what NotebookLM generates?
Reading is a start, but active processing improves retention dramatically. Annotate, highlight, and most importantly, rewrite key concepts in your own words. The generation-effect shows that producing text leads to better memory than passive reading.
How detailed should I ask study guides to be?
Match the detail level to your purpose. For initial learning, request comprehensive guides. For review, ask for one-page summaries or "highest-yield points only." Be explicit about length and detail in your prompts.
Can NotebookLM help me create flashcards?
Yes! Ask for "flashcard pairs" with front and back content. You can then copy these into Anki, Quizlet, or your preferred flashcard app. Request clinical relevance on the back for better application.
How do I know if the study guide covers everything I need?
Compare the generated guide against your lecture objectives or syllabus. Ask NotebookLM follow-up questions about any gaps. You can also request: "What other important topics about [subject] should I know?"
