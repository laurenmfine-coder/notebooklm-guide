The Four Learning Styles

Audio Learners

You remember what you hear. Lectures stick with you. You explain things out loud to understand them. Audio Overviews are your superpower.

Visual Learners

You need to see it. Diagrams, charts, and color-coding help you understand. You think in images and spatial relationships.

Read/Write Learners

You learn through text. Reading and writing reinforce understanding. Notes, summaries, and written explanations are your tools.

Kinesthetic Learners

You learn by doing. Practice, cases, and hands-on activities cement knowledge. Sitting still and reading isn't enough.

Most People Are Multi-Modal

While you likely have a dominant style, most effective learners use multiple modalities. Each guide includes strategies for combining your primary style with others.

For example, an audio learner might:

  • Listen to an Audio Overview during their commute
  • Follow up with generated practice questions (read/write)
  • Create a visual summary of key points (visual)
  • Work through a clinical case to apply knowledge (kinesthetic)
