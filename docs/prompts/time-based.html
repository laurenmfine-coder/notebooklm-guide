Time-Based Prompts

Prompts optimized for 5-minute, 15-minute, 30-minute, and 1-hour sessions

Match your study approach to the time you have. These prompts are designed to generate appropriately-sized content for each time block.

5-Minute Study Session

Focus: Quick wins, single concepts, rapid review

Quick Concept Review

Best for: Refreshing a single concept

Give me a 2-3 minute explanation of [concept] focusing only on what I absolutely need to know.

Single Comparison

Best for: Quick differentiation

Briefly compare [A] and [B]. Focus on the key difference I need to know to distinguish them.

Quick Quiz

Best for: Self-testing

Ask me 3 quick questions about [topic] to test my understanding. Include the answers.

One Key Fact

Best for: Memorization

What's the single most important thing to know about [topic] for clinical practice?

15-Minute Study Session

Focus: Focused review, moderate depth, practice questions

Focused Study Guide

Best for: Targeted review of one topic

Create a focused study guide on [specific topic]. Include pathophysiology, presentation, and management. Keep it concise for 15-minute review.

Practice Questions Set

Best for: Active recall practice

Generate 5 practice questions about [topic] at [exam level]. Include explanations for each answer.

Concept Explanation

Best for: Understanding a tricky topic

Explain [complex concept] in detail. Break it down into clear steps and include clinical relevance.

Quick Case

Best for: Clinical application

Create a brief clinical case about [condition]. Include just enough detail for me to work through diagnosis and initial management.

30-Minute Study Session

Focus: Comprehensive topic review, multiple cases, deeper understanding

Comprehensive Topic Review

Best for: Thorough understanding of one topic

Create a complete study session on [topic]. Include: overview, key concepts, practice questions, and clinical applications.

Case-Based Learning

Best for: Clinical reasoning practice

Generate 3 clinical cases about [topic] at different difficulty levels. Include questions for each and detailed explanations.

Audio Overview + Review

Best for: Multi-modal learning

Create a detailed audio overview of [topic] suitable for a 20-30 minute study session. Include multiple examples and clinical applications.

Comparison Deep Dive

Best for: Distinguishing similar conditions

Create a comprehensive comparison of [list 3-4 related conditions]. Include a detailed table plus clinical pearls for differentiating them.

1+ Hour Study Session

Focus: Deep learning, integration, comprehensive mastery

Deep Dive Study Package

Best for: Comprehensive topic mastery

Create a comprehensive learning package for [topic] including: detailed study guide, multiple practice cases, 10+ practice questions, and an audio overview. Organize for systematic studying.

Integration Session

Best for: Connecting related topics

Create a study session that integrates [topic A] with [topic B] and [topic C]. Show connections, generate cases requiring integration, and create comprehensive review materials.

Exam Topic Mastery

Best for: Thorough exam preparation

Help me master [topic] for [specific exam]. Include: comprehensive overview, high-yield facts, common test questions, clinical pearls, and practice cases.

Organ System Review

Best for: Systems-based studying

Create a complete review of the [organ system] including: anatomy, physiology, common pathology, pharmacology, and clinical applications. Include practice questions for each section.
Pro Tip

Even with limited time, active engagement beats passive reading. A 5-minute session with practice questions will stick better than 15 minutes of re-reading notes. Match your prompts to encourage active learning regardless of time available.
