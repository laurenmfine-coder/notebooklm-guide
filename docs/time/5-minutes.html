5-Minute Quick Wins

Maximum impact in minimum time

The Power of 5 Minutes

You have more 5-minute windows than you realize: between classes, waiting for the elevator, in line for coffee, before a meeting starts. Each one is a learning opportunity.

What You Can Do in 5 Minutes

1. Quick Q&A Review

  • Open a notebook you've already created
  • Ask one question about content you're studying
  • Read the response
  • Quiz yourself: could you have answered it?

Time: 3-5 minutes

2. Audio Micro-Session

  • Listen to 5 minutes of an audio overview
  • Pause
  • Summarize what you heard (mentally or out loud)

Time: 5 minutes exactly

3. Single Concept Review

  • Pick one concept you learned recently
  • Try to explain it out loud or in your head
  • Check against NotebookLM if needed

Time: 3-4 minutes

4. Flashcard-Style Q&A

  • Ask NotebookLM to "quiz me on [topic]"
  • Answer the question
  • Check your answer
  • One question = one micro-session

Time: 2-3 minutes per question

Setting Up for 5-Minute Success

Preparation (Do This Once)

  1. Bookmark NotebookLM on your phone
  2. Download audio files for offline access
  3. Create a "Quick Review" notebook with current week's content

Ready-to-Go Questions

Save these prompts for instant use:

  • "What are the 3 most important points about [topic]?"
  • "Quiz me on [topic] - one question"
  • "Summarize [concept] in 2-3 sentences"
  • "What's the mechanism of [drug/disease]?"

5-Minute Windows Throughout Your Day

When Best Activity
Before class starts Quick Q&A on upcoming topic
Between classes Audio micro-session
Waiting for elevator Single concept recall
In line for food One flashcard question
Before bed Review one thing from today
