Half-Day Study Sessions

2-4 hours for comprehensive work

When You Have Real Time

A half-day block (2-4 hours) is precious. Use it for work that can't happen in shorter sessions: building systems, comprehensive review, or intensive exam prep.

Half-Day Session Types

Type 1: System Building (2-3 hours)

Build infrastructure that serves you for months:

  1. Hour 1: Create comprehensive notebook for one subject. Upload all relevant sources (lectures, textbook chapters, notes)
  2. Hour 2: Generate and review study guides for each major topic
  3. Hour 3: Generate audio overviews. Download and organize for future listening

Outcome: Complete study system for one subject

Type 2: Exam Preparation (3-4 hours)

Intensive review before a block exam:

  1. Hour 1: Review comprehensive study guide for entire block
  2. Hour 2: Q&A deep dive on weak areas identified from practice questions
  3. Hour 3: Practice questions with NotebookLM for explanations
  4. Hour 4: Final audio review of high-yield topics

Outcome: Comprehensive exam preparation

Type 3: Weak Spot Elimination (2-3 hours)

Target your most challenging topics:

  1. Hour 1: Identify 3 weakest topics from practice questions. Create focused notebook
  2. Hour 2: Deep Q&A on each topic. Ask "why" until you understand
  3. Hour 3: Test yourself. Generate new questions. Verify understanding

Outcome: Transform weaknesses into strengths

Structuring Long Sessions

Take Real Breaks

  • 25-30 minute work blocks
  • 5 minute breaks (walk, stretch, water)
  • 15-20 minute break after 2 hours

Vary Your Activities

Don't do the same thing for 4 hours:

  • Mix reading, listening, writing, and testing
  • Alternate between creating content and reviewing content
  • Include both passive and active learning

Fuel Appropriately

  • Eat before you start (not a heavy meal)
  • Have snacks available
  • Stay hydrated
  • Avoid energy crashes

Half-Day Scheduling

When to schedule half-day sessions:

  • Weekend mornings (fresh brain)
  • Days off
  • Reading week
  • Week before exams
