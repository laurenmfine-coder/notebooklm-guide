Advanced Gem Strategies

Power user techniques for maximum effectiveness

Multi-Notebook Workflows

You can attach multiple NotebookLM notebooks to a single Gemini conversation. This enables powerful cross-topic synthesis.

When to Use Multiple Notebooks

  • Connecting concepts: Attach Physiology + Pathology + Pharmacology notebooks to see how a disease connects across disciplines
  • Interdisciplinary research: Attach literature from multiple fields for novel connections
  • Course integration: Attach materials from prerequisite and current courses
  • Comparative analysis: Attach guidelines from different organizations or countries

Example Prompt

With Cardiology, Pulmonology, and Pharmacology notebooks attached:

"How does heart failure affect pulmonary function, and what pharmacologic considerations arise from treating both systems?"

The Gem synthesizes across all three notebooks with citations from each.

Organization Tip

Keep notebooks focused on single topics. It's easier to combine focused notebooks than to navigate one massive notebook with everything.

Iterating on Instructions

Your first version of a Gem won't be perfect. Here's how to refine it:

The Iteration Loop

  1. Use the Gem for a real task
  2. Note what it does well and poorly
  3. Identify the specific behavior you want to change
  4. Add explicit instructions addressing that behavior
  5. Test again

Common Fixes

Problem Fix to Add
Gives answers when I want questions "NEVER give answers directly, even if I explicitly ask"
Too verbose "Keep responses concise. Use bullet points. Maximum 3 paragraphs."
Too terse "Provide detailed explanations with examples and analogies."
Sounds like generic AI "Match the voice and style of my attached writing samples exactly."
Doesn't cite sources "Always cite specific sources from my attached materials."
Too easy on me "Be demanding. Point out problems directly. Don't soften criticism."

Using Web Search with Notebooks

Gems can search the web while also using your NotebookLM materials. This is powerful for:

  • Finding updates: Your attached guidelines are from 2023, but you need current recommendations
  • Filling gaps: Your notebook covers treatment but not epidemiology—search for current data
  • Verification: Check if something in your sources is still accurate

How to Prompt for This

"Based on my attached materials AND current web sources, what are the latest recommendations for [topic]?"

The Gem will combine grounded information from your notebook with current web results.

Caveat

Web search results aren't as reliable as your curated NotebookLM sources. The Gem may find outdated or incorrect information online. Use web search to supplement, not replace, your verified materials.

Gem Chaining

Use multiple Gems in sequence for complex workflows:

Example: Research to Publication

  1. Literature Synthesizer → Identifies themes and gaps in your reading
  2. Methods Critic → Strengthens your study design
  3. Grant Writing Partner → Helps frame for funders
  4. Critical Editor → Polishes the final manuscript
  5. Voice Matcher → Ensures it sounds like you

Example: Course Development

  1. Curriculum Aligner → Maps objectives to activities
  2. Case Developer → Creates teaching cases
  3. Quiz Master → Generates assessment items
  4. Student Support Bot → Deployed for student use

Advanced Instruction Techniques

Role + Rules + Reminders

Structure your instructions in three parts:

  • Role: Who is the Gem? ("You are a demanding editor...")
  • Rules: What must it always/never do? ("NEVER give praise without specific evidence...")
  • Reminders: What should it check at the end? ("Before responding, verify you've cited sources...")

Conditional Behaviors

Make the Gem adapt to different situations:

"If I say 'quiz me,' generate questions. If I say 'explain,' teach the concept. If I say 'debrief,' help me reflect on what I learned."

Output Formatting

Specify exactly how you want responses structured:

"Format all feedback as: (1) What's working, (2) What needs improvement, (3) Specific next steps. Use bullet points. Keep each section under 5 bullets."

Troubleshooting

Gem Ignores Instructions

  • Make instructions more explicit ("NEVER" instead of "try not to")
  • Put the most important rules first
  • Repeat critical instructions at the end

Gem Doesn't Use My Notebook

  • Explicitly say "reference my attached NotebookLM materials"
  • Ask "what do my sources say about [topic]?"
  • Check that the notebook is actually attached (look for the attachment indicator)

Responses Are Too Generic

  • Add more writing samples to your notebook
  • Include instructions about what to avoid ("no corporate jargon, no clichés")
  • Specify the exact tone you want

Current Limitations

Keep these in mind as you build your workflow:

  • Web only: NotebookLM integration works in Gemini web, not mobile app (yet)
  • Static sources: NotebookLM notebooks don't auto-update—you must add new sources manually
  • Read-only: Gems can analyze but can't edit your documents or take actions
  • Gradual rollout: Not everyone has access yet; Pro/Ultra accounts typically get features first
  • Context limits: Very long conversations may lose earlier context

Your Next Steps

Beginner: Create one Gem, attach one notebook, use it for a week. Note what works and what doesn't.

Intermediate: Create 3-4 Gems for different purposes. Experiment with multi-notebook conversations.

Advanced: Build a complete workflow using Gem chaining. Share a Student Support Bot with your class.
