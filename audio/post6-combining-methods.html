Combining Audio with Other Methods

Multi-modal strategies

Audio Is Your Strength, Not Your Limit

While audio is your primary channel, combining modalities strengthens retention. Lead with audio, supplement strategically.

Audio + Visual

When: Anatomy, spatial content, diagrams

  1. Listen to audio overview first (conceptual framework)
  2. Then review visuals (images, diagrams)
  3. Return to audio while mentally visualizing

Audio + Writing

When: Complex material, exam prep

  1. Listen to audio overview
  2. Pause and write key points
  3. Listen again while reviewing notes

Audio + Practice

When: Preparing for application

  1. Listen to audio on topic
  2. Do practice questions
  3. Re-listen focusing on areas you missed

Your Personal Mix

Suggested ratio for audio-dominant learners:

  • 60-70% audio-based learning
  • 15-20% visual supplement
  • 10-15% writing/practice

Experiment

This week, try one "audio + visual" session and one "audio + writing" session. Note which combination helps retention for different content types.
